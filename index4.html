My first web site

In 1928, Alexander Fleming, a Scottish bacteriologist, made an accidental yet groundbreaking discovery.
While researching staphylococcal bacteria, he left his lab in disarray during a vacation. Upon returning,
he noticed something unusual: a mold, later identified as Penicillium notatum, had contaminated one of his
petri dishes. Strangely, the bacteria around the mold were no longer growing. This moment of serendipity led to the development of penicillin, the world’s
first true antibiotic. During World War II, penicillin saved countless lives by
treating infections that were previously fatal. Its discovery marked the beginning of the antibiotic era,
revolutionizing medicine and offering hope against bacterial diseases.
